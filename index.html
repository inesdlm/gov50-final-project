Project Proposal

What is the relationship between the GDP growth rate of Spain and the formation of separatist movements or revolutions? I believe that if GDP growth is stagnant or negative, there would be a small rise in separatist movements. In “How Democracies Die” by Ziblatt and Levitsky, they stated that the better the economic state of a nation, the less likely there is to be democratic breakdown. If this is true, then I am curious to see if it also increases or decreases national unity and discontent, using separatist movements and revoutions as a sort of proxy. The data set I will be using is the political violence data set provided on the course website. Thus, my explanatory variable is the real GDP growth rate. I think this will provide me a good amount of variation in the variable. The outcome that I am interested in exploring based on variations in GDP growth is separatist movements and revolts. It is measured in the number of separatist movements in 1975, and revolution by forced changes in the government elite in the 70s. To support my hypothesis, two relationships could be true. First, a negative or stagnant growth of GDP coupled with a rise in separatist movements and revolutions. Alternatively, a positive GDP growth and low number of separatist movements or revolutions. A pattern of growth coupled with a rise in separatist/revolutionary sentiment would disprove my hypothesis, as well as its inverse. I am interested in exploring this relationship, or something similar to it, in order to understand politics and my own Spanish background better.
